Bobby Charlton 1956–1973

One of the club's most legendary figures, he made 758 appearances and scored 249 goals. His leadership helped secure three league titles and a European Cup victory in 1968. Recognized as a key player in England's 1966 World Cup win, he remains an influential figure in football history.

George Best 1963–1974

Known for his exceptional skill, he made 470 appearances and scored 179 goals. Best played a crucial role in securing two league titles and the 1968 European Cup. His charisma and talent on the field left a lasting impact on the sport and inspired many future generations.

Ryan Giggs 1990–2014

The record holder for most appearances, he played 963 matches and scored 168 goals. Over his career, he won 13 Premier League titles and 2 UEFA Champions League trophies. His consistency and longevity make him a vital part of the club's success in the modern era.

Paul Scholes 1993–2011

Making 718 appearances and scoring 155 goals, he was a key figure in the midfield. His vision and passing ability contributed to 11 Premier League titles and 2 UEFA Champions League wins. Scholes is remembered for his intelligence and influence on the pitch.

Cristiano Ronaldo 2003–2009, 2021–2022

He made 292 appearances and scored 118 goals during his first stint with the club. His time at United included three Premier League titles and a UEFA Champions League trophy. Ronaldo returned to contribute further, reaffirming his status as one of football's all-time greats.

Wayne Rooney 2004–2017

As the club's all-time leading goalscorer with 253 goals in 559 appearances, he played a versatile role in the attack. His tenure included five Premier League titles and a UEFA Champions League win, marking him as a pivotal player in the club's history.
